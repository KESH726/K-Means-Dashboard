What the model is telling us
Key findings & recommended actions from the real cluster data
The model reveals a funnel with a near-total blockage at the top. The Awareness cluster (93.8%) is characterised by passive, single-profile sessions — users land on a Speaker page, browse briefly, and leave without exploring further. The Consideration cluster (4.9%) is a radically different user: they trigger 51.9 events and visit 9.9 pages, yet still don't convert. These are deep researchers who need a conversion trigger, not more content. The Conversion cluster (1.1%) is the most revealing — they use fewer pages (4.4) and fewer events (21.6) than Consideration users, meaning they arrive with intent already formed.

The critical insight: Speaker Profile pages are the dominant landing page across all three stages (72.8%, 48.6%, 76.1%). The page isn't the problem — what happens after it is. Awareness users don't go further. Consideration users explore but hit no conversion trigger. Only a tiny fraction arrive ready to act.
AWARENESS ACTION

Add strong next-step prompts directly on Speaker Profile pages — related speakers, curated lists, or a "see availability" CTA — to push beyond the 1.4-page average.

CONSIDERATION ACTION

These users have done the research. Deploy exit-intent overlays, demo request CTAs, or limited-availability signals on high-dwell pages to trigger the conversion decision.

CONVERSION ACTION

Converters arrive ready — don't interrupt them. Optimise the Speaker Profile → booking path for speed and reduce friction on the request/enquiry form.
